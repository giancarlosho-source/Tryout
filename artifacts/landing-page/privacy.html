Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

TryoutDesk ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of the organizations and individuals who use our platform. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

1. Information We Collect

Account information. When you sign up, we collect your organization name, email address, and password (stored as a secure hash). We also collect payment information processed on your behalf by Stripe — we do not store raw card numbers.

Player and evaluation data. As part of using the Service, you may upload or enter information about players, including names, ages, contact information, evaluation scores, and photos. This data is entered by you and belongs to your organization.

Usage data. We collect information about how the Service is used, including log data (IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps) and device information, to operate and improve the Service.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide, maintain, and improve the Service
  • To process payments and manage your subscription
  • To send transactional emails (account setup, password resets, trial reminders)
  • To respond to your support requests
  • To detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents
  • To comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your data or the data of players entered into your account to third parties.

3. Player Data and Your Responsibilities

You act as the data controller for any personal information you enter about players, coaches, or other individuals. You are responsible for obtaining any required consent from those individuals and for complying with applicable privacy laws (including FERPA, COPPA, GDPR, or state privacy laws as applicable).

We process player data solely on your instructions as a data processor and do not use it for any purpose beyond delivering the Service to you.

4. Data Sharing

We share data only with the following categories of third parties, solely to operate the Service:

  • Stripe — payment processing and subscription management
  • Resend — transactional email delivery
  • Railway — cloud infrastructure and database hosting
  • Vercel — frontend hosting and content delivery

All third-party providers are bound by their own privacy and security obligations. We do not share your data with advertisers or data brokers.

5. Data Retention

We retain your account data and Your Data for as long as your account is active. If you cancel your account, we will retain your data for up to 90 days to allow for account recovery, then delete it from our systems. You may export your data at any time through the Export feature in the Service before cancellation.

6. Security

We use industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS), bcrypt password hashing, and access controls. No security system is perfect; we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

7. Cookies

TryoutDesk uses browser localStorage (not cookies) to store your authentication token and session preferences. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking pixels.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is designed for use by sports organizations, not directly by minors. If player data you enter includes information about individuals under 13, you are responsible for ensuring appropriate consent has been obtained from their parents or guardians in compliance with applicable law (such as COPPA in the United States).

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or export the personal data we hold about you. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at support@tryoutdesk.com. We will respond within 30 days.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes by email or by a prominent notice within the Service. Continued use of the Service after changes become effective constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
